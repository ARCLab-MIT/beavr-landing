Abstract

BeaVR is an open-source, end-to-end teleoperation pipeline that leverages affordable hardware for robotics teleoperation. Building upon Open Teach, we address its accessibility limitations and improve upon their bimanual teleoperation methodology to create a more robust and user-friendly system.

Our system enables VR teleoperation out-of-the-box through Meta Quest 3 with low-latency control and visual feedback while recording synchronized proprioceptive, visual, and action data. BeaVR provides multi-embodiment support with drivers for RX-1 humanoid and xArm + LeapHand work-cells, and maintains simulation parity in MuJoCo and Isaac Gym for domain-randomized policy training.

The system is designed to be accessible with no proprietary hardware requirements, modular with clean Python and ROS modules, and community-driven with LeRobot formatted data collection. BeaVR enables capture of single-hand, bi-manual, or whole-body demonstrations for manipulation, assembly, or locomotion tasks using only commodity PCs and student-budget friendly hardware.

VR Teleoperation

Hand poses captured by Meta Quest 3 are converted into robot motion control commands for real-time teleoperation. BeaVR prioritizes accessibility and safety through affordable hardware, collision avoidance, and low-latency control, enabling robots to perform complex bimanual manipulation tasks safely in teleoperation mode.
